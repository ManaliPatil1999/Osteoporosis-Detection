INTRODUCTION TO OSTEOPOROSIS

OSTEOPOROSIS

Osteoporosis is a health condition that weakens bones, making them fragile and more likely to break. It develops slowly over several years and is often only diagnosed when a fall or sudden impact causes a bone to break (fracture).


INTRODUCTION

Osteoporosis is a condition which is characterized by low bone mineral density leading to high risk of fractures. It can wreak havoc on your dental health. The bone loss in the jaw from osteoporosis is linked to additional tooth loss. In fact, women with osteoporosis are three times more likely to have tooth loss than those without the condition. Thereâ€™s also evidence that bone loss in the jaw leads to gum disease, which can be serious and result in infections and tooth loss.

PREVENTION

Women typically start out with lower bone density than men, and loss of estrogen over time can increase the risk for osteoporosis. With the increasing age, loss in bone mineral density can be identified by thinning and porosity of mandible. This systemic disease is not curable but its prevention at early stage is recommended.

PURPOSE

Traditionally, osteoporosis was detected using dual energy X-ray absorptiometry (DEXA) test which is costlier and rarely done. As compared to DEXA test, OPG images are less costly. An OPG is a panoramic
X-ray of the upper and lower jaws, including the teeth.
